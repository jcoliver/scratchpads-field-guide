Nomenclature:

  • Bembidium horni Hayward, 1897: 116

Diagnostic Description: Specimens of Bembidion horni are darker (in both body and appendages) and broader than all other members of subgenus Liocosmius other than B. "SierraStar" . The pronotum is particular is wider than in most other Liocosmius. In color, the side margins of the elytra are dark brown to black, as is the background color of the pronotum and elytra. The antennae are dark brown to black, with only the basal three articles being at palest a reddish color. The femora are dark brown or dark reddish brown in most specimens. The only species that B. horni is likely to be confused with is B. "SierraStar". The pale elytral spots are smaller than in most Liocosmius, but not as small as those of B. "SierraStar". The posterior spots of B. horni are tapered toward the midline, and bent posteriorly, so that they are somewhat comma-shaped, whereas those of B. "SierraStar" are blunt medially. As well, the parascutellar stria of B. horni have distinct punctures that are much more evident than those of B. "SierraStar". The male genitalia of B. horni has a distinctive ventral bulge, and the flagellum of the internal sac is short, although not as short as B. "SierraStar".

Similar species: B. "SierraStar"

Size: Body length is 3.2 - 4.1 mm, with larger specimens being found east of California.

Distribution: This species lives in North America from Colorado and New Mexico west through Arizona and Utah to southern California. There are also populations in northern Baja California in Mexico.

Habitat: As is typical for members of subgenus Liocosmius, B. horni occurs on sand or silt shores of creeks and rivers, especially where the bank is steep, and more frequently in shaded areas.
